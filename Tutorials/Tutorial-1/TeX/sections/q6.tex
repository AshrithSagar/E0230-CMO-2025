\section*{Question 6}

Show that the function \( h(x, y)=x^{4}-4 x y+y^{4} \) has a global minimum.
What is that global minimum?

\subsection*{Solution}

Given the function \( h: \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) defined as
\begin{equation*}
    h(x, y) = x^4 - 4xy + y^4
\end{equation*}
we can compute its gradient \( \nabla h(x, y) \) and Hessian \( \nabla^2 h(x, y) \) as follows
\begin{align*}
    &
    \nabla h(x, y)
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \rfrac{\partial h}{\partial x} \\
        \rfrac{\partial h}{\partial y}
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        4 x^3 - 4y \\
        -4x + 4 y^3
    \end{bmatrix}
    \\ &
    \nabla^2 h(x, y)
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \rfrac{\partial^2 h}{\partial x^2} & \rfrac{\partial^2 h}{\partial x \partial y} \\
        \rfrac{\partial^2 h}{\partial y \partial x} & \rfrac{\partial^2 h}{\partial y^2}
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        12 x^2 & -4 \\
        -4 & 12 y^2
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}

The quadratic form associated with the Hessian is given by
\begin{equation*}
    \mathbf{z}^\top \; \nabla^2 h(x, y) \; \mathbf{z}
    =
    12 x^2 z_1^2 + 12 y^2 z_2^2 - 8 z_1 z_2
\end{equation*}
for any \(
    \mathbf{z} =
    {
        \begin{bmatrix}
            z_1 & z_2
        \end{bmatrix}
    }^\top
    \in \mathbb{R}^{2}
\).

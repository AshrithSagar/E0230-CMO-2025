\section*{Question 1}

Is the product of two positive definite matrices positive definite?
Why or why not?

\subsection*{Solution}

No, this is not necessarily true.

We know that \( \mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{A} \mathbf{x} > 0 \) and \( \mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{B} \mathbf{x} > 0 \) for all non-zero vectors \( \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n \) if \( \mathbf{A} \) and \( \mathbf{B} \) are positive definite matrices.
This does not imply that \( \mathbf{x}^\top (\mathbf{A} \mathbf{B}) \mathbf{x} > 0, \; \forall \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n \setminus \{ \mathbf{0} \} \).

\paragraph{Counter examples}
\begin{equation*}
    \mathbf{A} =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 0 \\
        0 & -1
    \end{bmatrix}
    , \quad
    \mathbf{B} =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 1 \\
        1 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    , \quad
    \mathbf{A} \mathbf{B} =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 1 \\
        -1 & -1
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation*}

Eigenvalues of \( \mathbf{A} \) are \( 1, -1 \) and eigenvalues of \( \mathbf{B} \) are \( 2, 0 \).
Eigenvalues of \( \mathbf{A} \mathbf{B} \) are \( 0, 0 \).

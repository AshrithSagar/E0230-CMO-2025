In the following, let \( \mathbf{I} = \left[\mathbf{e}_{1}, \ldots, \mathbf{e}_{d}\right] \) be a \( d \times d \) matrix with \( e_{j} \) be the \( j \)th column.
Also \( \mathbf{x} = {\left[x_{1}, x_{2}, \ldots, x_{d}\right]}^{\top} \in \R^{d} \) and \( \Vert \mathbf{x} \Vert = \sqrt{\mathbf{x}^{\top} \mathbf{x}} \).
Set of real symmetric \( d \times d \) matrices will be denoted by \( \mathcal{S}_{d} \), the set of positive semidefinite matrices will be denoted by \( \mathcal{S}_{d}^{+} \), and the set of positive definite matrices will be denoted by \( \mathcal{S}_{d}^{++} \).
\( [n] \) will denote the set \( \{1,2, \ldots, n\} \).
Denote \( \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{a}, \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \R^{d} \).
For a function \( f: \R^{d} \rightarrow \R, f \in \mathcal{C}_{L}^{1} \) the Algorithm \texttt{DESCENT} will refer to the following iteration
\begin{equation*}
    \mathbf{x}^{(k+1)}
    =
    \mathbf{x}^{(k)} + \alpha_{k} \mathbf{u}^{(k)}
    \tag{\texttt{DESCENT}}
\end{equation*}
where \( \mathbf{u}^{(k) \top} \nabla f\left(\mathbf{x}^{(k)}\right)<0 \).
